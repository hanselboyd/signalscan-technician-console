# SignalScan Safety Rules

## Core Safety Principle

SignalScan v1 is read-only.

It may inspect, summarize, explain, and report. It must not automatically change the client system.

## Mandatory Rules

1. Do not delete files automatically.
2. Do not modify registry keys automatically.
3. Do not disable services automatically.
4. Do not uninstall applications automatically.
5. Do not modify firewall rules automatically.
6. Do not modify antivirus or Defender settings automatically.
7. Do not install, remove, or change drivers automatically.
8. Do not claim malware removal.
9. Do not collect passwords.
10. Do not collect browser history.
11. Do not collect personal files, documents, photos, or emails.
12. Do not transmit client data to the cloud unless the technician explicitly enables that feature and the client consents.
13. Do not run hidden background tasks.
14. Do not make repairs without clear technician confirmation.
15. Do not present AI output as a final diagnosis without technician review.

## AI Safety Rules

AI may:

- Summarize scan findings
- Explain risks in plain English
- Draft client-facing report language
- Suggest service recommendations
- Help technician write notes

AI may not:

- Make system changes
- Execute repair commands
- Delete data
- Disable security settings
- Diagnose hardware failure with certainty
- Make medical/legal/financial-style guarantees
- Present uncertain findings as certain

## Technician Approval Rule

Any recommendation generated by AI must be editable by the technician before export.

## Report Disclaimer

Every report should include a disclaimer similar to:

> SignalScan provides a technician-reviewed system health assessment based on available diagnostic information at the time of scan. Findings are intended to guide maintenance and support decisions. Some issues may require additional hands-on inspection, hardware testing, vendor tools, or follow-up service.

## Development Safety

During development:

- Use test machines or virtual machines.
- Never test destructive features on client systems.
- Keep the v1 app read-only.
- Log diagnostic collection errors safely.
- Avoid requiring administrator privileges unless absolutely necessary for read-only checks.
- Clearly mark checks that require elevated permissions.

## Build/Release Safety

Before any release:

- Confirm all v1 diagnostic commands are read-only.
- Confirm no file deletion paths exist.
- Confirm no registry write paths exist.
- Confirm no service modification paths exist.
- Confirm PDF reports do not expose sensitive data unintentionally.
- Confirm local scan history can be cleared by the technician.
